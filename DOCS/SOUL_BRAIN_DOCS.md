# SOUL + BRAIN — Character Framework

> An attempt at a lightweight system for giving AI a consistent, stereotype-free understanding of who a character is.

---

## The Character Block

```
Name: [Character Name]

SOUL {
  Drive:   [two words — core motivational type]
  Surface: [two words — dominant wing, visible behavior]
  Depth:   [two words — dormant wing, hidden layer]
  Growth {
    Simple: [two words — natural growth direction, flows easily]
    Hard:   [two words — challenging growth direction, most rewarding]
  }
}

BRAIN: [MBTI type]
```

This is all you need. This goes at the top of your character sheet. What comes after is yours. The rest of the sheet is however you normally build a character. SOUL and BRAIN are just the starting point. The character fleshes out further from there on their own.

---

## SOUL — The Motivational Core

> The Enneagram gives AI costumes. My theory is that the moment AI sees a type number, it reaches for the archetype instead of the motivation underneath. So SOUL is a deliberate rename. The numbered types become **Cores**, identified only by their two words. The word "Enneagram" cannot appear in context. One mention and it collapses back into archetypes. AI may still recognize the underlying psychology, and that is fine. What matters is that the word never gives AI a shortcut.
>
> BRAIN carries less of that risk. But the rename still matters. It tells AI this is cognitive style only. How a character thinks, not who they are.

Each field uses exactly **two words** pulled from the master Core list. No extra description needed. AI reads the words and immediately knows how to write the character.

---

### The Nine Core Descriptors

These are the building blocks of SOUL. Every field pulls its two words from this list.

| Core | Two Words |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | Integrity, Correction |
| 2 | Needed, Love |
| 3 | Significance, Success |
| 4 | Identity, Compassion |
| 5 | Understanding, Observation |
| 6 | Trust, Connection |
| 7 | Possibility, Freedom |
| 8 | Power, Resolve |
| 9 | Ease, Harmony |

**Notes on specific cores:**

**Core 6 — Trust, Connection** — Trust captures the core anxiety — the need for safety and certainty. Connection captures the active ongoing maintenance of bonds in all directions — including across enemy lines.

**Core 8 — Power, Resolve** — Power points outward at what the 8 projects into the world. Resolve points inward at the unbreakable core nobody gets to touch. The tension between these two words is where the type lives.

**Core 9 — Ease, Harmony** — Ease points at the drive — the active need for undisturbed frictionless flow. Harmony carries the fuller meaning of being in accord with self and world simultaneously.

### How the Fields Work

**Drive** — The core type. Fixed for every character of that type. This is what the character is fundamentally chasing. Not a conscious goal — a deep orientation toward the world.

**Surface** — The dominant wing. Always one of the two adjacent cores on the circle. This is how the Drive visibly manifests in behavior day to day.

**Depth** — The dormant wing. The other adjacent core. Real, present, but quieter. Can surface at specific story moments — often the most revealing ones. The dormant wing is not inactive — it lives underneath everything and emerges when the story demands it.

**Growth** — Both directions are positive. Both are genuine character development. Neither is bad. The difference is only in how easy or hard they are to reach.

- **Simple** — growth that flows naturally and accessibly. The character moves here almost automatically. Low resistance. Use freely in any scene where the character develops or finds relief.
- **Hard** — growth that requires real effort and pushing beyond comfort. This is where the deepest transformation happens. The most rewarding direction. For some characters this may be permanently blocked — in which case it becomes the source of their central conflict rather than a path of growth.

---

### Key Principle — Words Describe Capacity, Not Morality

The two words for each core describe what a character is **capable of** — not whether they use it for good or evil.

A Core 4 character has **Identity, Compassion** as their Drive whether they are:
- Someone who holds space for others' pain
- Someone who reads people's deepest wounds and uses them as weapons

SOUL gives AI the raw material. The story context determines how it is used.

### When to Use Each Field

**Drive + Surface + Depth** — Use for any scene. This is the baseline character.

**Growth Simple** — Use when writing scenes where the character develops naturally, finds relief, or flows without much resistance. This direction is always accessible.

**Growth Hard** — Use when writing character transformation arcs, climax resolutions, turning points, scenes where the character pushes beyond themselves. This is where real and earned change happens.

## BRAIN — The Cognitive Style

SOUL tells AI *why* the character moves. BRAIN tells AI *how* the character thinks.

BRAIN takes a single MBTI type. AI already understands MBTI cognitive styles well enough that one label gives it everything it needs about how the character processes information, makes decisions, and engages with the world.

```
BRAIN: INTJ
```

SOUL and BRAIN together give AI a complete psychological picture — motivational depth and cognitive style — before a single word of story is written.
